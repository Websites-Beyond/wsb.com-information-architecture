heading: Anti-Patterns to Eliminate
---

We train ourselves to spot and fix system breakdowns — early and often:

- Repeating the same manual task more than once
- Critical knowledge living only in someone's head
- One-off solutions that can't be reused
- Inconsistent naming, file structure, or workflows
- Lack of documentation or unclear hand-offs
- Building around exceptions instead of designing for patterns
- Rushed fixes that ignore root causes

These are signals. When we see them, we pause and ask:

**Can this result be standardized? Can this process be systematized?**

> "An hour lost at a bottleneck is an hour lost for the entire system."
> — *Eliyahu Goldratt, The Goal*
